A Front-End Developer’s Journey: FOMO — #1

Published today by Sanjay

Fearing the things that I might’ve missed out
This is for people who are like me, a developer who barely scratched the surface of the world of Software Development and aims to gain mastery through efforts and repetition.

Have you ever been to a situation where you’re trying out new technology and by the time you are comfortable using it, it changes to a new version? (Yes, Angular, I’m looking at you!) Sucks right? The fear of missing out is kinda real, for me, I guess?

By the time this blog has been published, there’s already a plethora of technologies to choose from when creating products. Software development world is constantly changing. As a developer, you should be prepared to face these constant changes.

As part of my day-to-day developer tasks, I have countless tabs and websites to consult whenever I encounter issues with my code. Whether it’s a bad coding pattern or it’s just that I’m too noob to do it, there’s always an open Chrome tab to help me find the answers. The thing is, whenever I find a solution, I always go down the rabbit hole and realize that I don’t have a deep understanding of what I’ve been doing and fearing that I’m missing on a lot of things. Rinse. Repeat.

Am I competitive enough to keep up with the trend?

Ask yourself this in the next 3-6 months without exploring new things or improving your mastery over one thing. If the answer is yes, then you’re doing well, if not, then there’s a need for some adjustments. Here are some of what I think you and I should be doing:

1. Determine the market trend

Is it a new JavaScript framework? A new library? A new tech stack? Whatever it is, there will always be a piece of fundamental information about it. That leads to the second step:

2. Understand the basics

Frameworks and libraries are made from what I call a “Mother language”, and if you don’t understand these languages of your choice, you won’t be able to fully utilize these said technologies to their fullest. What’s better, “Jack of all trades”, or “master of one”? I think the latter sounds better to me.

3. Read and explore

After choosing the trend you decided to take, consume the knowledge behind it and put it into practice. There’s no better way to understand things than to practice using it.

Fear is Good

Yep. You read it right. I think that fear is essential for us, not only as a developer but as a human being. Fearing over something that you might’ve missed out pushes you to evaluate yourself if you’re going to the right path of the road you chose to take.
